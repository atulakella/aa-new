About Me.

Hey there! I'm Atul Akella, an Electronics and Communications Engineering student, but more importantly, I'm a maker at heart. I build things that I find cool. I’ve worked on real-time projects using ESP32, Raspberry Pi, and NVIDIA Jetson boards, applying my programming skills in C++ and Python to bring ideas to life. Robotics and AI fascinate me, and I’m constantly working to expand my expertise in these areas. I also have solid experience with ROS2, which has been a game-changer in a couple of my projects.Being an engineer means more to me than just coding—I take pride in designing, creating, and solving real challenges. Beyond ECE, I’m exploring machine learning, backend development, APIs, CAD design, 3D printing, Generative AI, LLMs, and Networking. Plus, I’m a big fan of self-hosting.

When I’m not working on projects, I love participating in hackathons and CTFs, and as a freshman. If you’re into tech or just want to chat about cool projects, feel free to reach out. Let’s connect!

Projects.

The Ghee Project

Moved by the Tirupati laddu controversy, where beef tallow was reportedly mixed into the sacred prasadam, I started developing an electronic nose to differentiate between pure desi ghee and adulterated ghee. So far, I've achieved 68% accuracy and am actively working on improving its precision.

Brain Activity Monitoring

I developed a brain activity monitoring project that differentiates between states like meditation, normal activity, studying, and gaming. This exciting work has opened up new avenues for exploration, and I'm actively continueing to building on top of this.

Plant Sense

PlantSense is a user-friendly, web-based solution designed to help farmers identify and manage plant diseases without the need for expensive technology such as drones, thermal sensors, or specialized hardware. Farmers can simply upload an image of their plant, and our platform—powered by YOLOv11.
